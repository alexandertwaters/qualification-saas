Auditor Appendix
System Classification:
The Resolver is a non‑validated, advisory software system used to support preliminary equipment qualification planning. It operates upstream of formal quality systems and does not participate in controlled document management, approval workflows, execution, or record retention.

The Resolver is not a system of record and does not replace or modify existing quality system controls.

Use in Regulated Environments:
The Resolver is used to generate draft qualification obligations based on declared equipment attributes, capabilities, and intended use. Outputs are reviewed, approved, and executed by qualified personnel using established organizational procedures.

The Resolver does not execute qualification activities, evaluate acceptance criteria, or determine compliance. All qualification decisions remain the responsibility of the regulated organization.

Relationship to Quality Systems:
The Resolver operates independently of quality systems. It does not interface with, depend upon, or integrate into quality system workflows.

Outputs generated by the Resolver may be manually incorporated into controlled documents, such as qualification protocols, at the discretion of the organization and in accordance with its quality system procedures.

Standards References:
The Resolver references industry standards to provide contextual traceability for qualification obligations. Standards references are included to support understanding of why an obligation exists.

The Resolver does not interpret standards, determine applicability, or assert compliance. Standards references are expressed using clause identifiers, titles, and original paraphrased summaries.

The Resolver does not store, access, distribute, or reproduce copyrighted standards content. Authoritative standards publications must be consulted directly.

Data Handling and Execution State:
The Resolver does not monitor operational performance, runtime behavior, or execution outcomes. Outputs are generated independently of historical qualification results or in‑use data.

The Resolver does not store executed protocols, approvals, evidence artifacts, or operational records.

Change Control and Stability:
Qualification obligations produced by the Resolver are assigned stable, human‑readable identifiers that are permanently bound to a specific semantic meaning.

Obligation meanings are immutable once published. When practices evolve, new obligations are introduced and prior obligations are deprecated rather than altered, preserving historical interpretability.

Resolver behavior and outputs are governed by a versioned contract. Minor updates preserve all existing meanings and guarantees. Major version updates are required for any change to scope, purpose, or interpretive authority.
